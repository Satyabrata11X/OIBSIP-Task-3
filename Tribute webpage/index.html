Swami Vivekananda

A Voice That Echoed Across the World in 1893

About

Swami Vivekananda was a Hindu monk and one of the most influential spiritual leaders of India. Born in 1863, he played a major role in the revival of Hinduism in India and introduced Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He is best known for his powerful speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he began with the iconic words, "Sisters and Brothers of America." A disciple of Sri Ramakrishna, Vivekananda believed in service to humanity as service to God and founded the Ramakrishna Mission to promote education, healthcare, and spiritual growth. His teachings continue to inspire people to lead lives of strength, compassion, and purpose.

Timeline / Achievements

  • 1863 - Born as Narendranath Datta in Kolkata, India
  • 1881 - Met his spiritual teacher, Sri Ramakrishna
  • 1886 - Took monastic vows and became Swami Vivekananda after Ramakrishna's passing
  • 1893 - Gained international fame with his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago
  • 1897 - Founded the Ramakrishna Mission to serve society through education and social reform
  • 1899 - Established the Belur Math, a monastery and headquarters of the Ramakrishna Order
  • 1902 - Passed away at the young age of 39, leaving behind a legacy of spiritual and national awakening
